# Skill: Synthesis

## Purpose

Connect ideas across sources, disciplines, experiences, and long-term themes.

Synthesis is for building an idea network rather than treating each learning moment as isolated.

## When to Use

- The user asks how two books, concepts, or fields relate.
- The user wants to build a larger framework or personal thought system.
- Multiple sources seem connected but the relationship is unclear.
- The user notices recurring themes across learning.
- The user wants to compare perspectives without flattening their differences.

## When NOT to Use

- The individual concepts are not yet understood.
- The user mainly wants to remember one source.
- The user wants immediate practical application.
- The connection would be forced, shallow, or decorative.
- The user needs conceptual distinction before integration.

## Default Method Sequence

1. `question-driven-learning`: identify the synthesis question.
2. `concept-mapping`: list and relate the concepts or sources.
3. `distinction`: preserve differences before integrating.
4. `synthesis`: build the higher-level connection or model.
5. `counterexample`: test whether the synthesis overreaches.
6. `progressive-summarization`: compress the synthesis into a usable takeaway.

## Optional Method Add-ons

- `analogy`: when two domains share a useful structure.
- `first-principles`: when the synthesis should return to foundational assumptions.
- `inversion`: when reversing the frame exposes hidden tensions.
- `metacognition`: when the synthesis reveals a recurring personal theme.
- `transfer`: when the synthesis should be applied to a real context.

## Memory Outputs

- Updated Idea Network.
- Cross-source links.
- Recurring themes in the Learning Profile.
- New higher-level models.
- Open tensions between ideas.

## Exit Conditions

- A meaningful connection has been named.
- The relationship between sources is clearer than before.
- The synthesis has been tested against at least one example or boundary.
- The user decides the concepts require deeper individual understanding first.
- The synthesis becomes too broad and needs narrowing.

## Example Scenarios

- "How does this book connect to The Joy of Living?"
- "Is Buddhist attachment similar to psychological addiction?"
- "I want to connect Buddhism, psychology, and AI learning."
- "How does game theory relate to everyday negotiation?"
- "These two authors seem to disagree. Can we compare them?"
