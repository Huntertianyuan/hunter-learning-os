# Method: Socratic Questioning

## Purpose

Deepen understanding by questioning assumptions, reasons, evidence, implications, and alternatives.

This method helps the learner discover the structure of their own thinking rather than passively receiving an answer.

## When to Use

- The user holds a belief, interpretation, judgment, or conclusion that needs examination.
- The user is unsure why they agree or disagree with an idea.
- A hidden assumption may be shaping the user's understanding.
- The user wants deeper insight rather than quick information.

## When NOT to Use

- The user is tired, resistant, or has signaled they do not want deep questioning.
- The conversation has already become too questionnaire-like.
- The user needs grounding, summary, or emotional space first.
- The system already has enough evidence to answer directly.

## Inputs

- A claim, belief, interpretation, or reaction.
- User's stated reasons.
- Source context, if relevant.
- Any known emotional or cognitive sensitivity.

## Workflow

1. Identify the claim or assumption being explored.
2. Ask one open, high-value question at a time.
3. Focus on one dimension: meaning, evidence, cause, implication, alternative, or consequence.
4. Reflect the user's answer before asking another question.
5. Offer an exit or turn after several rounds.
6. Stop when a clearer assumption, distinction, or open question emerges.

## Outputs

- Clarified assumptions.
- Stronger or revised reasoning.
- Exposed tensions or contradictions.
- Open questions for later reflection.

## Success Criteria

- The user feels helped, not interrogated.
- A hidden assumption becomes visible.
- The user's reasoning becomes clearer or more nuanced.
- The conversation reaches a natural landing point.

## Memory Integration

- Save major assumption shifts as Cognitive Change History candidates.
- Update Learning Profile with recurring reasoning patterns.
- Record unresolved but important questions.
- Update Misconception History if questioning corrects a misunderstanding.

## Compatible Skills

- Deep Dive
- Synthesis
- Application
- Reflection
